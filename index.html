Section 2

The Three-Wave Disruption Framework

AI in financial services is not a single event. It is three overlapping waves, each demanding different skills and creating different opportunities.

Wave 1: Copilots Everywhere (2024–2025)

Internal LLM platforms are already deployed at scale. JPMorgan onboarded 200,000 users in 8 months; Goldman Sachs rolled out its GS AI Assistant to 10,000+ employees. Drafting, summarizing, and data analysis are the first tasks transformed.

Wave 2: Agentic Workflow Automation (2025–2027)

Deloitte forecasts 25% of GenAI-using enterprises will deploy AI agents by 2025, rising to 50% by 2027. Klarna replaced the work of 700 FTEs with agentic customer service. Multi-step task completion replaces simple drafting.

Wave 3: System-Level Effects (2028–2030)

Goldman Sachs estimates a $780B total addressable market for AI in financial services. Regulators flag correlated model behavior and common-provider dependency. Governance roles expand even as routine tasks shrink.
