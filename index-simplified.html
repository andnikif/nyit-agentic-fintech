Section 2 — The Gap

Three Waves of AI Disruption in Finance

AI in financial services is not a single event. It is three overlapping waves, each demanding different skills and creating different opportunities.

Wave 1: Copilots Everywhere (2024–2025)

JPMorgan onboarded 200,000 users in 8 months. Goldman Sachs rolled out its GS AI Assistant to 10,000+ employees. Drafting, summarizing, and data analysis are the first tasks transformed.

Wave 2: Agentic Automation (2025–2027)

Deloitte forecasts 25% of enterprises will deploy AI agents by 2025, rising to 50% by 2027. Klarna replaced the work of 700 FTEs with agentic customer service. Multi-step automation replaces simple drafting.

Wave 3: System-Level Effects (2028–2030)

Goldman Sachs estimates a $780B total addressable market for AI in financial services. Regulators flag correlated model behavior. Governance roles expand even as routine tasks shrink.

The Insight: One Gap, Not Four

A Financial Analyst, a Compliance Officer, and a Risk Manager face the same AI skill gap. They all need to understand how AI works, all need to build AI-powered workflows, and all need governance frameworks for regulated environments. The domain knowledge they already have — credit analysis, regulatory compliance, portfolio management — is their career. What they're missing is the AI layer.

This is why the certificates are organized by AI skill progression, not by financial role. You already know finance. We teach you the AI.
